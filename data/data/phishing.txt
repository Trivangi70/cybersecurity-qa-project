Phishing is a cyber attack that attempts to trick individuals into giving away sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, and credit card details by pretending to be a trustworthy entity. It often involves fake emails or websites that look legitimate.

There are several types of phishing:
1. Email Phishing – Fake emails pretending to be from reputable companies.
2. Spear Phishing – Targeted attacks on specific individuals or organizations.
3. Whaling – Attacks targeting high-profile individuals like executives.
4. Smishing – Phishing through SMS.
5. Vishing – Voice call phishing.

To prevent phishing:
- Never click on suspicious links.
- Verify email addresses and website URLs.
- Use multi-factor authentication.
- Keep software up to date.
